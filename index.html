About Dan Kelley

Dan is a Professor of Oceanography at Dalhousie University in Halifax, Nova Scotia, Canada. He teaches there, and is also the Graduate Coordinator. His research focuses on ocean mixing, especially that caused by internal waves and double-diffusive convection. Some information about Dan and his work can be found at the following links.

  1. Dalhousie University provides contact information and (by university mandate) mentions just five publications.
  2. ResearchGate provides free PDFs of publications, along with (rmarkably inaccurate) citation counts.
  3. Google-scholar provides publisher links (which require login) to some publications, along with (somewhat accurate) citation counts.

Dan is the author of the Oce package for oceanographic analysis in R, and readers can follow its development, along with other things he makes public, by viewing his Github repositories.

In late 2013, Dan started experimenting with blogging, creating a WordPress blog. Finding the WordPress data entry scheme being somewhat arduous, and disliking the lock-in to a commercial site, he started experimenting with github blogging in early 2014, yielding the entries provided below.
